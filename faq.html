Pricing & Cost

Residential garage floors in Scottsdale typically run $3.50 to $8.50 per square foot depending on the system, size of the space, and current floor condition. Here's a general breakdown:


• Standard flake system (most popular): $3.50–$5.00/sq ft
• Metallic epoxy: $5.50–$8.50/sq ft
• Solid color / quartz: $3.00–$4.50/sq ft
• Commercial warehouse: $2.50–$4.50/sq ft


A standard 2-car garage (~500 sq ft) with a flake system typically runs between $1,750 and $2,500 installed. We provide exact written quotes after an on-site visit — no phone guessing.

The biggest price difference comes down to prep method and material quality. Contractors who acid-wash (instead of diamond-grind) and use water-based or solvent-cut epoxies can quote $1.50–$2.50/sq ft — but those floors frequently peel within 1–3 years.


We use professional-grade 100% solids epoxy, diamond-grinding equipment, and moisture testing on every job. It costs more upfront, but the floor lasts 15–20+ years instead of 2–3. Our 5-year warranty reflects that confidence.

Installation & Process

Most residential garage floors are completed in a single day. Here's a typical timeline:


• Day 1 (7am–4pm): Diamond grind entire surface, edge detailing, crack repair, primer coat, base coat, decorative flake broadcast, full polyaspartic topcoat
• 24 hours: Stay off the floor completely
• 48–72 hours: Light foot traffic OK
• 7 days: Safe for vehicles


Metallic epoxy systems and commercial jobs typically require 2+ days due to additional cure windows between coats.

Diamond grinding uses industrial diamond-segment heads attached to a propane or electric grinder to mechanically profile the concrete surface. The grinding removes surface contamination, laitance (weak surface layer), and old coatings — and creates millions of microscopic pores that the epoxy bonds into.


Acid washing — what many budget contractors use — creates a chemical etch rather than a mechanical profile. It's weaker, less consistent, and doesn't remove contamination. It also generates harsh fumes and creates disposal challenges.


The statistics speak for themselves: the vast majority of epoxy failures trace back to inadequate surface preparation. Diamond grinding is non-negotiable on every Camelback Epoxy job.

Durability & Maintenance

Properly installed epoxy floors with diamond-grind prep and quality materials last 15–20+ years under normal use. Our polyaspartic topcoat is UV-stable, so it won't yellow in the Arizona sun (a common problem with cheaper UV-reactive epoxy topcoats).


We back every installation with a 5-year written workmanship warranty — the best in the Scottsdale market.

Standard epoxy with a smooth topcoat can be slippery when wet. That's why we include a light aluminum oxide anti-slip additive in our topcoat on all residential garage floors by default — it provides excellent traction without noticeably affecting the floor's appearance.


For commercial applications (restaurants, warehouses), we use a heavier grit additive and can customize the slip resistance level to meet OSHA or health code requirements.

Epoxy floors are remarkably easy to maintain:


• Daily/weekly: Sweep or dust mop to remove grit
• Mopping: Warm water with a pH-neutral cleaner (we recommend simple dish soap). Avoid vinegar, citrus cleaners, or any acidic cleaner.
• Stains: Clean spills promptly. Oil, grease, and automotive fluids wipe up easily with a degreaser.
• Annual: Consider a professional inspection and light cleaning around Year 3–5 to assess topcoat condition


We provide a maintenance guide with every completed job.

Arizona heat actually highlights the importance of using quality materials. Cheap water-based epoxies and solvent-cut products can soften, discolor, or delaminate under extreme heat — especially in garages that reach 130°F+ in summer.


Our 100% solids epoxy systems and UV-stable polyaspartic topcoats are specifically engineered for high-heat environments. They won't yellow, soften, or delaminate due to Arizona temperatures. This is one of the main reasons we refuse to use budget materials.
